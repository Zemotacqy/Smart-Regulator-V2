Based on the regulatory text provided in the image, here is an elaborate breakdown of the conditions that investors must satisfy to be eligible for investing in Restricted schemes:

1. General Investment Thresholds
To be eligible, an investor must generally fall into one of the following baseline categories:

Minimum Investment: The investor must invest at least USD 150,000.

Accredited Status: The investor must be recognized as an Accredited Investor.

Note: The final proviso explicitly states that the minimum investment threshold (like the USD 150,000 requirement) does not apply to an accredited investor.

2. Special Concessions for Internal Personnel
There is a lower investment threshold for individuals closely tied to the Fund Management Entity (FME):

Eligible Roles: Employees, directors, or designated partners of the FME.

Reduced Minimum: For these specific individuals, the minimum investment value is lowered to USD 40,000.

3. Joint Investor Conditions
The regulations allow multiple investors to pool their resources as joint investors under two distinct provisos:

Condition A: General Joint Investors
An FME can accept investments from multiple individuals acting together as joint investors.

Each individual investor within this group must invest at least the minimum applicable investment amount (e.g., USD 150,000 for standard individuals or USD 40,000 for FME employees/directors).

Condition B: Immediate Family Joint Investors
A specific exception is made for close family pairings. A group of not more than two individuals can act as joint investors where their combined total investment is at least USD 150,000, provided the pairing consists of:

An investor and his/her spouse

An investor and his/her parent

An investor and his/her daughter/son

In this specific family scenario, the individual members do not each need to meet the USD 150,000 mark, as long as their combined joint pool reaches it.

4. Overall Scheme Cap (Investor Limit)
While not an individual qualification criterion, the scheme itself must satisfy a macro-eligibility condition regarding its investor base:

A Restricted scheme cannot have more than one thousand (1,000) investors, or a specific limit otherwise designated by the Authority.

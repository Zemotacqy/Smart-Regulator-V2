Based on the document provided in Screenshot 2026-06-17 at 1.49.32 PM.png, subject to other regulatory provisions, a restricted scheme can invest only in specific instruments or entities within the IFSC, India, or a foreign jurisdiction.

The permissible investment options are classified as follows:

Core Investment Instruments and Entities
Unlisted securities

Securities listed or to be listed or traded on stock exchanges

Money market instruments

Debt securities

Securitised debt instruments, which must be either asset-backed or mortgage-backed securities

Units of other investment schemes, provided appropriate disclosure is made in the placement memorandum

Derivatives, including commodity derivatives, subject to suitable disclosures in the placement memorandum

Limited liability partnerships

Any other securities, financial products, assets, or instruments as specified by the Authority

Temporary Deployment of Funds
Pending deployment of monies, a Fund Management Entity (FME) is permitted to invest in certificates of deposit, units of investment schemes (such as overnight, liquid, or money market schemes), and other money market instruments.
